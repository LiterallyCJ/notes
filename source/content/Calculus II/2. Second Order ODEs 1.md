$$
\frac{d^2y}{dt^2} = f(\frac{dy}{dt}, y, t)
$$
This is a second order equation because the highest order of the derivative is 2. 

Linear second order ODE:
$$
\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + p(x)\frac{dy}{dx} +q(x)y = r(x)
$$
If $r(x) = 0$, the ODE is homogeneous.

The solution will be in the form
$$
y(x) = c_1y_1(x) + c_2y_2(x)
$$
given two linearly independent complementary functions $y_1$ and $y_2$

If it is inhomogeneous ($r(x) \neq 0$), the solution will be in the form
$$
y(x) = y_p(x) + c_1y_1(x) + c_2y_2(x)
$$
where $y_p$ is a particular integral which is the solution of the homogeneous form of the ODE.
