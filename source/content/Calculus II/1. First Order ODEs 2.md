## Solving First Order ODEs

### Method 1: Direct Integration

$$
\frac{dy}{dx} = f(x)
$$
Can be solved by simply using the anti-derivative of $f(x)$ and adding a constant to the end e.g

$$
y = \int^x f(s)\ ds + C
$$
### Method 2: Separable Variables

$$
g(y) \frac{dy}{dx} = h(x)
$$
The method is to rewrite the integral as:
$$
g(y)\ dy = h(x)\ dx
$$
and then integrate both sides
$$
\int g(y)\ dy = \int h(x)\ dx
$$
### Method 3: Linear First-Order ODEs

$$
\frac{dy}{dx} + p(x)y = q(x)
$$
To solve this, we need to use an integrating factor.
Let $P(x)$ be the antiderivative of p(x). Then multiply the whole equation by $e^{P(x)}$.
So we get,
$$
e^{P(x)} \frac{dy}{dx} + e^{P(x)} p(x)y = e^{P(x)}q(x)
$$
Notice that the left side is in product rule form, so we have
$$
\frac{dy}{dx}[ye^{P(x)}] = q(x)e^{P(x)}
$$
and now we integrate both sides to get
$$
ye^{P(x)} = \int q(x)e^{P(x)}\ dx
$$
