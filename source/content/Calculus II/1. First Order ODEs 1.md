A differential equation is an equation relating to one or more functions, their derivatives and the variables they depend on.
A solution to a differential equation is a function or set of functions that solve the equation for all values of the independent variables.

An ODE is a differential equation involving a single independent variable as well as an unknown function and its derivatives.

When the independent variable is modelling time, $t$ is used and dots will denote derivatives.

When the independent variable is not modelling time, primes will be used to denote derivatives.

The order of a differential equation is the order (e.g first, second, third derivative) of the highest derivative that appears in the equation. The degree of the differential equation is the power of the highest derivative involved.

A differential equation is called linear if the function $f$ is a linear function of $y$ and its derivatives.

To specify a unique solution to a differential equation, additional data must be given. For an ODE of order $n$, exactly $n$ conditions must be specified. 
If those conditions are given at a single point $x_0$ then the combination of the ODE and the conditions is called an IVP (Initial Value Problem), and the conditions are called initial conditions. 
If the conditions are given at two points $x_0$ and $x_1$, then we have a BVP (Boundary Value Problem) and the conditions are called boundary condiitons.
