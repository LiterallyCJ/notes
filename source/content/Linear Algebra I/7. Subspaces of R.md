<u>6.28</u>
Vectors $\vec{v_1}, \dots, \vec{v_k} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ are linearly dependent if and only if the equation $A\vec{x} = \vec{0}$ has a non zero solution $\vec{x} = \vec{c} \in \mathbb{R}^k$, where $A$ is the matrix with column vectors $\vec{v_1}, \dots, \vec{v_k}$

Equivalently, $\vec{v_1}, \dots, \vec{v_k}$ are linearly independent if and only if the equation $A\vec{x} = \vec{0}$ has only the trivial solution $\vec{x} = \vec{0} \in \mathbb{R}^k$

<u>6.29</u>
Let $\vec{v_1}, \dots, \vec{v_n} \in \mathbb{R}^n$, and let $A = (\vec{v_1}, \dots, \vec{v_n})_{n√ón}$ be the square matrix with column vectors $\vec{v_1}, \dots, \vec{v_n}$. Then $\vec{v_1}, \dots, \vec{v_n}$ are linearly independent if and only if $det(A) \neq 0$.
