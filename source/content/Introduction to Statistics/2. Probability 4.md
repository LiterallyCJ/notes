$A$ and $B$ are independent if $P(A|B) = P(A)$
Alternatively:
$$
\begin{align*}
P(A|B)&= \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} = P(A) \\
&= P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B)

\end{align*}
$$
If $A$ and $B$ are independent, so are $A'$ and $B'$

Proof:
$$
\begin{gather*}
\text{Want to show:} \\
P(A' \cap B') = P(A') \cdot P(B') \\
\end{gather*}
$$
$$
\begin{align*}
P(A' \cap B') &= P((A \cap B)') \\
&= 1-P(A \cap B) \\
\end{align*}
$$
